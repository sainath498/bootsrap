Nandhi hills

Nandi Hills (Anglicised forms include Nandidurg and Nandydoorg) is an ancient hill fortress built by Ganga Dynasty and later enlarged and strengthened by Tipu Sultan in southern India, in the Chikkaballapur district of Karnataka state. It is 10 km from Chickballapur town and approximately 60 km from the city of Bengaluru. The hills are near the town of Nandi. In traditional belief, the hills are the origin of the Arkavathy river,[1] Ponnaiyar River, Palar River,[2] Papagni and Penna River.[3] Watching the sunrise at Nandi Hills is popular with tourists.[4] The first ever SAARC summit hosted by India was held at Nandi Hills in 1986

Summer in Nandi Hills Summer in Nandi Hills Summer might not be the best time to visit Nandi Hills. Although the summers might not be all that bad, the other two seasons are comparatively better. The summer temperatures range between 23 degree Celsius to 40 degree Celsius. As long as you visit the hills in the months of March or in late May, you have nothing to worry about. But the peak summer temperatures could be quite hot and not ideal for trekking or visiting the hills. Depending on the day that you choose along with the temperature, you will be able to enjoy the warm breeze, the lush green milieu and a view incomparable! Just make sure to pack light and wear clothes that aren't too heavy. The sun could get a little harsh during the day so you might as well be prepared for it! Monsoon in Nandi Hills Monsoon in Nandi Hills Monsoon is often considered to be the ideal time for trekking and hiking. Come the rainy season and your heart wants to explore all the hilly terrains with their wide and alluring natural beauty! But would monsoon be the best time to visit Nandi Hills? Well, it might be quite tricky. For the adventure seekers out there, you would be amused by the torrential rains and downpour on the Nandi Hills. Provided you take all the necessary precautions and wear proper trekking gear, you would have a good time going up the hills. However, if you are fairly new to the activity and do not know much about trekking, it is not a safe option. People who wish to click pictures might also be let-down by the rainfall. But of course, if it is the adventure that you wish to relish, you would have a great time trekking up the Nandi hills during monsoon. pack yourself a good windcheater or a poncho, and you'll be set! Also, do not forget to wear proper trekking shoes that can withstand the slippery slopes without putting you in peril. Winter in Nandi Hills Winter in Nandi Hills Winter is easily one of the best times to visit Nandi Hills.
